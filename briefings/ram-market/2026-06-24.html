Wed, Jun 24, 2026

💾 RAM Market

DDR5 spot inches up to $46.667 (+0.36%) while server DRAM contract momentum strengthens and HBM4 supply strategies split

Spot & contract prices

  • DDR5 spot: DRAMeXchange print at Jun. 24 18:00 GMT+8 shows DDR5 16Gb 4800/5600 avg $46.667, +0.36% session. DDR5 16Gb eTT was $23.500, flat.
  • DDR4 spot: DRAMeXchange shows DDR4 16Gb 3200 avg $71.305, +0.95% session; DDR4 8Gb 3200 avg $35.900, flat session. TrendForce’s weekly spot note says mainstream DDR4 1Gx8 3200 rose 0.28% WoW to $35.90 as of Jun. 23, from $35.80.
  • Modules: DRAMeXchange weekly module board: DDR5 RDIMM 32GB 4800/5600 avg $1,275, +2.00% weekly; DDR5 UDIMM 16GB $209, flat; DDR4 UDIMM 16GB $149.924, -0.11% weekly.
  • Contract pricing: TrendForce’s Jun. 24 DRAM bulletin says server DRAM contract prices have upward momentum from robust server demand. Earlier TrendForce survey: conventional DRAM contract prices rose ~93–98% QoQ in 1Q26, with another +58–63% QoQ projected for 2Q26.
  • LPDDR5/5X: No fresh spot/contract price print found today. TrendForce still frames LPDRAM supply as structurally tight: NVIDIA reduced next-gen platform memory capacity due to LPDRAM bottlenecks, while suppliers prioritize high-margin AI/server applications.
  • HBM3/HBM3E/HBM4: No public HBM3E spot price. HBM remains allocation-driven. Samsung’s HBM4 revenue reportedly surpassed $1B four months after mass production and is projected above $1.2B by end-June; TrendForce raised Samsung’s 2026 HBM4 shipment outlook from 3.5B Gb to 4B Gb. SK hynix’s 2026 HBM4 outlook was revised down from 4.5B Gb to 4B Gb as its meaningful ramp shifts to 3Q26.

Supply side

  • Samsung: First-mover HBM4 ramp is ahead of competitors; Samsung is using a 4nm FinFET base die and has already shipped 12-layer HBM4E samples. HBM4E targets 14Gbps scalable to 16Gbps, 48GB 12-high, up to 3.6TB/s per stack, >20% performance uplift, and >30% capacity uplift vs prior generation.
  • SK hynix: Taking a more margin-optimized HBM4 approach; reportedly slowing selected HBM3E-to-HBM4 line conversions and shifting attention back toward commodity/server DRAM where near-term profitability is strong. Its 12-high HBM4E samples now ship to major customers at up to 16Gbps, 48GB, >20% better power efficiency, and 17% better thermal performance vs HBM4.
  • Micron: Earnings are due Jun. 24; investor focus is HBM progress, capex, and whether peak margins hold. TrendForce notes NVIDIA has approved all three major suppliers for HBM4 supply for Vera Rubin; Micron’s HBM4E ramp is still targeted for 2027, with TSMC expected to manufacture standard/custom logic dies.
  • Capacity / inventory: TrendForce says supplier inventories are at rock-bottom / extremely low levels because PC and server clients have been procuring aggressively. New cleanroom output is not near-term; 2026 bit growth is mainly from process migration and manufacturing optimization.
  • Controls / geopolitics: No new export-control shock found in the past 24–48h. Relevant overhang remains CXMT: TrendForce’s Jun. 17 bulletin said US military-list re-inclusion does not impose immediate sanctions, but Entity List constraints continue to limit advanced-node progress.

Demand side

  • AI / data center: Strongest driver. TrendForce says hyperscalers and server OEMs are actively adjusting capacity configurations and securing additional orders to preempt future constraints; AI/server demand is pulling supply toward high-capacity RDIMMs, LPDDR5X, and HBM.
  • PCs: Mixed but cost-pushed. PC OEM memory inventories are depleting quickly thanks to smooth processor deliveries and complete-system shipments; however, channel/module demand is weak. ASUS reportedly expects Q3 PC prices to rise ~5%, with cumulative PC price increases reaching ~30% since 4Q25 and possibly ~35% in Q3. Dell server prices were reported up 20–40% in related PC-pricing coverage.
  • Smartphones: Demand is the weak link. Smartphone brands are conservative due to soaring mobile DRAM costs; TrendForce’s mobile DRAM analysis says memory costs are causing sharp smartphone production forecast cuts and curbing consumer bit demand.
  • OEM inventories: Split: supplier inventory very low; PC OEM inventory depleting; module houses building inventory because channel demand is weak; smartphone brands risk reduced allocations later if they under-procure now.

1–2 week outlook

  • Tightening / firm for contract and server-linked DRAM; sideways-to-firm in spot. Buyers are resisting higher spot quotes, so spot trade may stay sluggish, but low supplier inventory plus hyperscaler/server procurement should keep price risk skewed upward.
