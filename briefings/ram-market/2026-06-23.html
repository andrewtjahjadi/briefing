💾 RAM Market

DDR5 spot up 0.72%; DDR4 16Gb jumped 1.27% while HBM remains allocation-led

Market signals

  • Spot prices — TrendForce/DRAMeXchange, updated 2026-06-23 18:10 GMT+8: DDR5 16Gb 4800/5600: session avg $46.50, ↑ 0.72%.
  • DDR5 16Gb eTT: $23.50, flat.
  • DDR4 16Gb 3200: session avg $70.634, ↑ 1.27%.
  • DDR4 16Gb eTT: $11.75, flat; DDR4 8Gb 3200: $35.90, flat; DDR4 8Gb eTT: $4.407, flat.
  • DDRWatcher cross-check: latest daily derived reads DDR5 $46.5000, DDR4 $41.1230, GDDR6 $11.5220; regime transition, spot momentum up but decelerating, no contract reset / no module pass-through seen.
  • Contract prices: no new public reset today. TrendForce’s public contract table still shows April/May framework; broader conventional DRAM pricing remains in a sharp upcycle: 1Q26 +93–98% QoQ, 2Q26E +58–63% QoQ, with customers accepting increases to secure allocations.
  • LPDDR5/5X / mobile DRAM: no fresh public daily price print found. TrendForce’s 2Q26 mobile DRAM note says smartphone mobile DRAM contract prices retain strong upward momentum; Samsung/Micron quoted first, SK hynix/CXMT tentative, and smartphone brands may cut 2026 production targets or miss LTA bit volumes as AI servers crowd out capacity.
  • HBM / HBM3E / HBM4: no public daily HBM quote found; status remains tight / allocation-led. Micron earnings preview: market is watching whether DRAM/HBM margins can hold; Micron’s HBM4E volume ramp is expected in 2027, with 1-gamma DRAM and TSMC logic dies.
  • NVIDIA has reportedly approved all three major suppliers for HBM4 supply for Vera Rubin.
  • SK hynix is shipping 12-high HBM4E samples to major customers: 48GB, up to 16Gbps/pin, >20% power-efficiency improvement, 17% thermal-performance improvement vs HBM4.
  • Samsung shipped HBM4E samples in late May; SK hynix followed mid-June; competition is shifting from HBM3E allocation to HBM4/HBM4E qualification.
  • Supply side: big-three DRAM vendors continue steering scarce bit output toward high-margin server DRAM/HBM. TrendForce says supplier inventories are extremely low, 2026 bit growth is mainly process-migration driven, and wafer-input growth is limited because new cleanrooms take time. Micron capex is rising sharply but meaningful output is not expected until 2H27–2028.
  • Demand side: AI/data-center remains the marginal buyer. CSPs are moving from AI-specific servers into general-purpose AI/inference servers, expanding demand beyond HBM3E, LPDDR5X, and high-capacity RDIMMs into broader RDIMM capacities. PC OEMs and smartphone vendors remain squeezed by limited DRAM availability and rising costs.
  • One-line outlook: Tightening to firm for the next 1–2 weeks; spot is still rising, but DDRWatcher’s “up decel / transition” flag says momentum may be less explosive unless another contract reset appears.
