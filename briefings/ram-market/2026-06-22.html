💾 RAM Market

DDR5 spot firms again; HBM4E race is the day’s key AI-memory signal

Market signals

  • Spot prices: TrendForce/DRAMeXchange spot table updated 2026-06-22 18:10 GMT+8: DDR5 16Gb 4800/5600: session avg $46.167, ↑ 0.36%.
  • DDR5 16Gb eTT: $23.50, flat.
  • DDR4 16Gb 3200: $69.75, ↑ 0.90%; DDR4 16Gb eTT: $11.75, ↓ 0.42%.
  • DDR4 8Gb 3200: $35.90, flat; DDR4 8Gb eTT: $4.407, ↓ 0.97%.
  • DDR3 4Gb: $11.152, ↑ 1.60% — mature-node tightness still visible.
  • Contract prices: TrendForce’s public DRAM contract page still shows 2H Apr data / May member-only detail; latest public conventional DRAM framework remains 1Q26 +93–98% QoQ and 2Q26E +58–63% QoQ, driven by customers accepting hikes to secure supply. DDRWatcher says no new contract reset or module pass-through signal yet this week.
  • LPDDR5/5X / mobile DRAM: no fresh public spot % print found. TrendForce’s mobile DRAM note says 2Q26 smartphone mobile DRAM contract prices are maintaining strong upward momentum; smartphone brands are struggling to absorb two quarters of steep hikes and may revise 2026 production / LTA procurement volumes lower as AI servers crowd out capacity.
  • HBM: no public daily HBM price print found. Status remains tight / allocation-led: Micron preview: market is watching 2026 HBM/DRAM margins, HBM4/HBM4E updates, and capex; TrendForce-cited piece says NVIDIA has green-lit all three major suppliers for HBM4 supply to Vera Rubin.
  • SK hynix shipped 12-high HBM4E samples to major customers: 48GB stack, up to 16Gbps per pin, >20% power-efficiency improvement, and 17% better thermal performance vs HBM4.
  • Samsung reportedly shipped HBM4E samples in late May; SK hynix followed in mid-June; Micron targets HBM4E volume ramp in 2027 using 1-gamma DRAM and TSMC logic dies.
  • Supply side: TrendForce says 2Q26 supplier inventories remain extremely low, incremental supply is prioritized to high-capacity RDIMMs for AI servers, and PC/smartphone availability is limited.
  • Big-three DRAM vendors continue prioritizing higher-priced server applications; 2026 bit growth is mainly from process migration, with limited wafer-input growth because new cleanrooms take time.
  • Micron capex is expected to exceed $25B in FY2026, but meaningful output additions are not expected until 2H27–2028.
  • China/tool-chain note: Japanese top-five chip-toolmakers’ China sales fell ~10–12% in FY25, with front-end tools down nearly 20% YoY; not an immediate DRAM supply shock, but it reinforces localization/geopolitical friction around capacity expansion.
  • Demand side: AI/data center remains the marginal buyer: CSPs are accepting price hikes to secure allocations; demand has broadened beyond HBM3E/LPDDR5X/high-capacity RDIMMs into more general RDIMM capacities as inference/general-purpose AI servers scale.
  • PC and smartphone OEMs remain squeezed by limited DRAM availability and rising component costs; smartphone brands may cut production targets or miss earlier LTA bit commitments.
  • DDRWatcher marks the market transition / low confidence, with spot momentum up but decelerating.
  • One-line outlook: Tight but less explosive over the next 1–2 weeks — spot still rising and supplier inventories remain low, but DDRWatcher flags decelerating spot momentum and no fresh contract reset signal.
