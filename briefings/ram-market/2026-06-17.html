💾 RAM Market

DDR5 spot climbs while AI-memory allocation stays tight

Market signals

  • DDR5 spot: the archived Jun. 17 read remains an upward DDR5 market, with mainstream DDR5 pricing around $45.40 and buyers still paying up for immediately available supply.
  • AI-memory allocation: HBM, LPDDR, and high-capacity server DIMM allocation stayed tight as suppliers prioritized AI-server demand over PC and smartphone elasticity.
  • Supplier mix: the useful signal was not just daily spot movement; it was the ongoing reallocation of scarce wafer output toward server and AI-memory products.
  • Near-term read: tight supply and AI-server demand kept the bias upward, but PC and handset buyers were already showing price resistance.

Why it matters

  • For AI infrastructure: HBM and high-capacity RDIMM tightness remained the constraint to watch, because small changes in allocation can move server BOM assumptions quickly.
  • For consumer hardware: PC and smartphone OEMs faced continued margin pressure as memory costs rose faster than end-market demand.
