🇮🇩 Indonesia Daily — Friday Jun 26, 2026

JCI sold off, the rupiah stayed under pressure, and policy focus split between BI defense, SOE-bank liquidity support, Danantara governance, and lower oil.

💱 Markets & Macro

  • IDR/USD: USD/IDR around 17,834.5 on Jun 26, down 0.75% on the session; Trading Economics also described the rupiah as having traded near 17,950 earlier Friday amid broad USD strength and Danantara-governance concerns.
  • JCI/IHSG: JCI around 5,896, down 1.72%; morning trade was broadly weak, with basic materials, cyclicals, and industrials among laggards. Named losers included Jasa Marga -7.6%, Darma Henwa -6.7%, Sarana Mitra Luas -6.2%.
  • 10Y INDOGB: 7.18%, flat on the day; up 0.47 pts over the past month.
  • Bank Indonesia: BI raised the BI-Rate 25 bps to 5.75% at the 17–18 Jun 2026 Board of Governors meeting; Deposit Facility 4.75%, Lending Facility 6.50%. BI framed the move as rupiah stabilization and inflation-prevention, while keeping macroprudential/payment policy pro-growth.
  • Oil: Brent $72.11/bbl (-4.19%) and WTI $69.29/bbl (-3.65%) on Jun 26. Lower oil is material for Indonesia: Finance Minister Purbaya said easing US-Iran tensions and lower crude could reduce non-subsidized Pertamax fuel prices and support H2 growth.

📊 Economic Data & Policy

  • Inflation/GDP: Finance Ministry cited Q1 2026 GDP +5.61% y/y and May CPI +3.08% y/y; no new consensus print found in the last 24–48h.
  • External buffers: BI reported end-May FX reserves of $144.9bn, equal to 5.6 months of imports or 5.5 months incl. government external-debt service. BI also said the April trade surplus narrowed to $0.1bn from $3.3bn in March; Finance Ministry separately said the surplus streak reached 72 months.
  • Liquidity/credit: BI’s May money data showed M2 +10.8% y/y to Rp10,415.9tn, up from +9.2% in April; disbursed loans grew 10.8% y/y vs 9.4% in April.
  • Manufacturing: May manufacturing PMI improved to 50.0 from 49.1 in April; Industry Ministry said the recovery reflected domestic-order resilience and inventory build-ups against imported-input cost/supply risks. Industrial Confidence Index rose to 53.56 from 51.75.
  • Fiscal/liquidity policy: Finance Minister Purbaya announced up to Rp400tn placement into state-owned banks/Himbara from SAL funds held at BI; ministry cited SAL of Rp590tn. The goal is to ease banking liquidity and sustain growth.
  • IMF offer declined: Finance Ministry said Indonesia declined an IMF loan offer of $20bn–$30bn, arguing fiscal fundamentals and domestic reserves were sufficient; Purbaya said Indonesia had nearly $25bn reserved for its own use.
  • MBG/free meals: Government is reviewing MBG governance, prioritizing 3B groups (pregnant women, breastfeeding mothers, toddlers) and 3T regions; separate reporting says 7,500 MBG kitchens have halal certification. Governance remains a live execution risk after BGN leadership changes.
  • Danantara/bonds: A revised P2SK provision gives special protection for primary-market purchases of Danantara’s Patriot Bond and Merah Putih Bond. Finance Ministry says protection applies only to funds invested, not investors’ other businesses; Antara opinion flagged FATF/moral-hazard and fiscal-credibility concerns.
  • Trade/tariffs/export-ban watch: No fresh nickel/palm/coal export-ban move found in the last 24–48h. Russia crude remains active: ESDM said contracts are being prepared for a gradual 150mn-barrel Russian oil import commitment through end-2026.

🏭 Business & Sector News

  • Banking/SOE: The planned Rp400tn Himbara liquidity placement is the day’s biggest SOE-financial-system story; it directly targets state-bank funding stress and credit transmission.
  • Gas/LNG: ESDM said the $20bn Abadi Masela gas project is scheduled to begin construction in 2027, with commercial production targeted 2029–2030; INPEX remains operator and FEED progress is positive.
  • Geothermal/energy transition: Dieng Geothermal Power Plant Unit 2 broke ground: 55 MW, targeted operation 2028, backed as part of Prabowo’s energy self-sufficiency mandate.
  • Russian oil: ESDM confirmed a Russian crude import contract is prepared; details such as volume, oil type, port, and payment mechanism still need technical confirmation, despite the stated 150mn-barrel framework.
  • Downstreaming/metals: Indonesia and Switzerland signed an MoU on mineral/metals downstream processing in Basel; the embassy framed it as Switzerland’s first cooperation arrangement specifically backing Indonesia’s downstreaming agenda.
  • Industrial policy: Airlangga said downstreaming in industrial estates/SEZs has pushed ICOR to about 3 vs national average 6, supporting the argument that downstream hubs are more investment-efficient.
  • Autos: Prabowo highlighted the Maung presidential vehicle as a first step toward national automotive independence; he cited 60–70% domestic content, while acknowledging it is not yet fully local.
  • Consumption/digital: Tourism/Trade ministries launched BINA Holiday & Back to School discounts up to 50%, covering 800 brands, ~80,000 outlets, 414 malls, 24 provinces. Creative Economy Ministry said Indonesia’s games market is about $2bn/Rp34tn, with 200mn+ active gamers but local developers still only 0.17–0.34% market share and 2.5% of value.

✈️ President Prabowo — Travel

  • Recent public domestic travel: 24 Jun 2026 — Gorontalo: attended PENAS KTNA XVII farmers/fishers peak ceremony in Limboto; received KTNA gold badge; pushed food/energy self-sufficiency and B50 biodiesel messaging.
  • Recent public domestic travel: 23 Jun 2026 — East Java/Madura: inaugurated 1,151 km of regional roads across 37 provinces and attended NU national conference/grand conference events in Bangkalan.
  • Jakarta meetings: 25 Jun 2026 — Jakarta Palace meetings on higher education/industrialization and National Police/security; not a travel signal, but relevant to domestic-execution messaging.
  • Upcoming / planned: n/a confirmed in public sources checked today.

📌 Bottom Line

  • Indonesia is tightening at the central-bank level while loosening through fiscal/SOE-bank liquidity — a deliberate mix to defend the rupiah without choking domestic credit.
  • Markets are punishing governance/fiscal-risk questions around Danantara even as lower oil could help the current account, fuel prices, and purchasing power.
  • Prabowo’s near-term travel signal is domestic execution: food/energy self-sufficiency, roads, NU outreach, and industrial capability rather than new foreign trips.
