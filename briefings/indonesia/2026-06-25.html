🇮🇩 Indonesia Daily — Thu Jun 25, 2026

JCI bounced nearly 2% and the rupiah eased, but BI remains defensive as MBG cuts, B50 rollout, and Danantara export controls shape the policy signal.

💱 Markets & Macro

  • IDR/USD: Rp17,953.9/USD on Jun 25, down 0.10% from the prior session in USD/IDR terms — a small rupiah bounce, but still under pressure above Rp17,900/USD.
  • JCI/IHSG: 5,999, +1.96% from the prior session, recovering from a three-day slide. Sector color from Trading Economics: all sectors advanced, led by transport, healthcare, energy, and infrastructure; standout movers included Barito Pacific +6.6%, Indah Kiat +4.9%, Astra +3.0%, Sumber Alfaria +2.5%.
  • 10Y INDOGB: 7.18%, +2 bps from the previous session; up 47 bps m/m and 55 bps y/y by Trading Economics OTC quotes.
  • Bank Indonesia: BI-Rate 5.75%, Deposit Facility 4.75%, Lending Facility 6.50% after the 17–18 Jun 2026 Board of Governors meeting; BI raised by 25 bps for rupiah stabilization and inflation-target defense.
  • Oil: Brent US$74.83/bbl, +1.48%; WTI US$71.51/bbl, +1.66%. Material for Indonesia because Hormuz/shipping risk keeps fuel-import and subsidy math sensitive just before B50 rollout.

📊 Economic Data & Policy

  • Latest macro dashboard: CPI 3.08% y/y vs 2.42% prior; CPI m/m 0.28% vs 0.13% prior; annual GDP growth 5.61% vs 5.39% prior; manufacturing PMI 50.0 vs 49.1 prior; consumer confidence 121 vs 123 prior; retail sales m/m -11.6% vs +10.3% prior. Consensus: n/a from retrieved public sources.
  • Trade: latest balance of trade shown at US$89.1m vs US$3.32bn prior on the public dashboard; consensus: n/a.
  • Money supply: BI reported May broad money M2 +10.8% y/y to Rp10,415.9tn, accelerating from +9.2% in April. M1 rose 15.3%, quasi-money 6.0%, disbursed loans 10.8%, and net foreign assets 5.2%.
  • BI policy plumbing: From 1 Jul 2026, BI will lift the Bank Foreign Funding Ratio cap to 40% of bank capital from 35%, keep SRBI tenor pricing aligned with the higher BI-Rate, and maintain heavier FX intervention via offshore NDF, spot, and DNDF.
  • Fiscal / MBG: Government cut the 2026 Free Nutritious Meals (MBG) budget to Rp268tn from Rp335tn, a Rp67tn reduction. As of Apr 30, disbursement was Rp75tn to 61.96m recipients and 27,952 kitchens; state spending was Rp1,082.8tn, revenue Rp918.4tn, deficit 0.64% of GDP / Rp164.4tn.
  • Fuel / subsidies: B50 remains the near-term policy pivot: Prabowo said Indonesia will launch the 50% palm-oil biodiesel blend in July; government estimate is up to Rp157.28tn in FX savings this year if implementation displaces diesel imports.
  • FDI / trade / export controls: Strategic commodity export governance is still shifting toward Danantara Sumberdaya Indonesia (DSI), but Customs says exporters can continue current procedures through 31 Dec 2026; mandatory single-window routing starts 1 Jan 2027. Initial commodities: crude palm oil, coal, ferroalloys.

🏭 Business & Sector News

  • SOE/BUMN / Danantara: DSI will eventually be exporter of record for selected strategic commodities, while underlying producers remain goods owners. Exporters must report activity to DSI during the transition; CEISA 4.0 and Indonesia National Single Window stay in use.
  • Palm oil / biodiesel: Pertamina Patra Niaga says nationwide distribution infrastructure is ready for B50 from Jul 1; fuel volume is expected to stay comparable to B40, with the composition shifting to 50% FAME / 50% petroleum diesel. Pricing will adjust to market conditions.
  • Coal / CPO / ferroalloys: Existing export procedures remain through 2026, but policy risk is rising: DSI’s future role includes single-gateway intermediation plus supervision over export controls, selling prices, and reasonable margin thresholds.
  • Nickel / copper / LNG: n/a for new high-signal public items in the last 24–48h from retrieved public sources.
  • Equities: The JCI rebound looks more like bargain-hunting after a sharp drawdown than a clean risk-on turn; foreign inflows into SRBI and government bonds are helping, but rupiah weakness and next week’s CPI/trade data remain the watch points.

📌 Bottom Line

  • Market tone improved today — JCI bounced nearly 2% and USD/IDR eased — but the rupiah is still weak enough to keep BI defensive.
  • The most important policy move is fiscal reprioritization: MBG was cut by Rp67tn, while B50 and Danantara’s commodity-export controls are still moving forward.
  • For investors abroad, Indonesia’s near-term risk is execution: keep watching rupiah stability, B50 logistics/pricing, and DSI export-rule clarity.
