🇮🇩 Indonesia Daily — Mon Jun 22, 2026

BI’s rupiah defense is still being tested as equities slip, yields rise, stimulus arrives, and Java power-security risks resurface.

💱 Markets & Macro

  • IDR/USD: USD/IDR around 17,834 on Jun 22, up 0.05% on the session; rupiah slightly weaker on the day and ~0.62% weaker m/m. Context: BI tightening and FX rules are limiting damage, but MSCI accessibility/classification risk and a strong dollar remain the pressure points.
  • JCI/IHSG: 6,116–6,117, down ~0.98%. Jakarta Globe reported the index closed at 6,116 after trading 6,052–6,226; decliners beat gainers 445 to 221. Sector pressure was broad, led by basic materials, cyclicals, and energy; notable laggards included Merdeka Battery Materials (-4.6%), Darma Henwa (-3.8%), Kalbe Farma (-2.9%), and BTN (-2.4%).
  • 10Y INDOGB: 7.13%, up 6 bp on the day; +43 bp m/m.
  • Bank Indonesia rate: BI Rate 5.75% after a 25 bp hike on Thu Jun 18, 2026; Deposit Facility 4.75%, Lending Facility 6.50%. BI framed the hike as rupiah stabilization and inflation preemption; it says Q2 foreign inflow was US$3.9bn through Jun 15 after US$0.8bn outflow in Q1.
  • Oil: Brent US$77.89/bbl (-3.35%) and WTI US$73.53/bbl (-4.92%) on Jun 22. Lower crude helps Indonesia’s subsidy/import bill at the margin, but Middle East headline risk is still feeding FX/equity volatility.

📊 Economic Data & Policy

  • Growth/inflation: Q1 2026 GDP printed 5.61% y/y; May CPI 3.08% y/y vs 2.42% prior; consensus n/a. Finance Minister Purbaya said Indonesia is outperforming G20/ASEAN averages while keeping inflation within target range.
  • Activity buffers: Antara cited PMI at 50.0, broad liquidity growth 14.8% y/y, bank lending 11.5% y/y, FX reserves US$144.9bn / 5.6 months of imports, and a trade surplus streak of 72 months.
  • H2 stimulus: Government launched a Rp26.34tn / ~US$1.5bn second-half package: Rp18.04tn for food assistance/price stabilization, Rp2.04tn transport incentives, Rp4.14tn apprenticeships for 150,000 participants, and Rp2.12tn vocational training for 270,000 people.
  • Food/subsidies: Rice aid: 10 kg/month for 32.24m beneficiaries for three months from July, budget Rp17.54tn. Soybean subsidy for tofu/tempeh: Rp2,000/kg on a 250,000-ton quota, budget Rp500bn.
  • Transport stimulus: Antara separately reported Rp1.54tn for school-holiday and year-end travel discounts, including 30% train/ship discounts and VAT borne by government on domestic economy air tickets.
  • Petrochemical/tariff policy: Indonesia set 0% import duty for LPG and plastic inputs for six months; officials estimate Rp2.25tn economic benefit vs Rp360bn/year forgone revenue, aimed at naphtha/plastic supply disruption from Middle East risk.
  • Commodities/trade: Pupuk Indonesia’s first 47,250-tonne urea shipment reached Brisbane under a 250,000-tonne Indonesia-Australia G-to-G deal; talks are underway with India, Bangladesh, and other Asian buyers. No fresh confirmed nickel/export-ban update found in the 24–48h scan.

🏭 Business & Sector News

  • Power/coal: PLN says Java’s power system is recovering after rolling blackouts. One of two major IPP plants has been synchronized back to the grid; coal supply matching plant specs is moving again. The stress point is medium-calorie coal around 5,200 kcal/kg GAR and the US$70/ton DMO price cap vs higher global prices.
  • Prabowo + PLN: PLN chief Darmawan Prasodjo reported to Prabowo at the Presidential Palace on Jun 22; Jakarta Globe cited a coal-contract gap of roughly 20m tonnes vs PLN’s 154m tonnes/year requirement.
  • Coal quota/labor: Kutai Timur is preparing retraining plans if national coal output is cut toward 600m tonnes from the 790m-tonne 2025 record. East Kalimantan accounts for roughly 55% of national coal output; local mining employment exposure is politically sensitive.
  • Danantara/BUMN: Setkab said Prabowo met Danantara head Rosan Roeslani on Jun 21 to push SOE transformation and asset optimization. Setkab cited 1,077 SOE entities, 258 already consolidated, and a near-term target of around 300.
  • State banks: Jakarta Globe cited Rosan saying Himbara state banks have combined market cap around Rp1.1 quadrillion / US$67.2bn, about 10% of IDX market cap, and are being positioned as development-finance engines for MSMEs and priority programs.
  • IPO pipeline: Jakarta Eye Center operator Nitrasanata Dharma (JECX) plans to raise up to Rp683.1bn; Inaco maker Niramas Utama (JELI) targets up to Rp392bn. Both are using proceeds for debt, capex/expansion, and working capital.
  • Palm oil/nickel/copper/LNG: No high-signal fresh 24–48h public-source item found for nickel, copper, or LNG. Palm oil watch remains domestic B50 timing and export/logistics pressure, but no new same-day number was verified in today’s source set.

📌 Bottom Line

  • Indonesia is paying for rupiah defense with higher rates: BI has done 100 bp of hikes, but markets still care more about MSCI risk, fiscal credibility, and Middle East/oil volatility.
  • The government is leaning fiscal-supportive despite currency pressure: Rp26.34tn in H2 stimulus targets food, transport, and jobs to keep household demand alive.
  • Power security is back in focus: Java blackouts exposed coal-spec/DMO-price friction, which matters for industry reliability and Prabowo’s SOE governance push.
