## Spot & contract prices

- **DDR5 spot:** DRAMeXchange front page latest print was **Jun. 18 18:00 GMT+8**: **DDR5 16Gb 4800/5600 session avg $46.000, ↑ 1.32%**. DDR5 eTT avg **$23.500, ↑ 1.29%**.
- **DDR4 spot:** DDR4 16Gb 3200 avg **$69.125, ↑ 0.91%**; DDR4 16Gb eTT **$11.800, ↓ 0.63%**; DDR4 8Gb 3200 **$35.900, ↑ 0.28%**; DDR4 8Gb eTT **$4.450, ↓ 0.58%**. Mixed but main branded DDR4 remains firm.
- **Module spot:** DDR5 UDIMM 16GB **$209.000, ↓ 0.95% weekly**; DDR5 RDIMM 32GB **$1,250.000, ↑ 16.28% weekly**; DDR4 UDIMM 16GB **$150.087, flat**. Server RDIMM pricing is still the stress point.
- **Contract prices:** Latest accessible TrendForce DRAM bulletin summary dated **Jun. 17** says **DRAM contract and spot markets maintain upward trends**; no public contract % reset was available in this run. DRAMeXchange contract pages require login.
- **LPDDR5/5X:** No fresh public spot number found. TrendForce’s smartphone/mobile DRAM summaries still point to **sharply elevated LPDDR4X/LPDDR5X quotes**, Korean/US suppliers tightening full-year smartphone allocations, and price-hike pace likely moderating in 2H26.
- **HBM3/HBM3E/HBM4:** No public daily price print. Status remains **tight / allocation-driven**. The new datapoint is **HBM4E samples**: SK hynix says it shipped **12-layer HBM4E** samples to major customers; Samsung shipped samples earlier on **May 29**. This shifts the race from announcement to customer qualification.

## Supply side

- **SK hynix:** Shipped **12-layer HBM4E samples**: **48GB per stack**, up to **16Gbps/pin**, roughly **4TB/s bandwidth**, **>20% better energy efficiency**, and **~17% lower thermal resistance vs HBM4** per company/press reports. This is not volume production yet; customer qualification decides 2027 AI platform orders.
- **Samsung:** Already sampled **12-layer HBM4E** on **May 29**, with matching **48GB / 16Gbps** headline specs; Samsung is emphasizing its in-house 1c DRAM + 4nm logic-base-die path.
- **Micron:** No new verified 24h production update found. Korea Herald reports Micron keeps the HBM4E race three-way and has said its HBM4 ramp is ahead of schedule, with **HBM4E mass production expected next year**.
- **Capacity allocation:** TrendForce’s Jun. 16 memory note says major memory suppliers are still prioritizing **HBM and advanced-layer 3D NAND**, squeezing mature-node capacity; this is showing up in NOR/SLC shortages but is also consistent with conventional DRAM tightness.
- **China/export controls:** TrendForce’s Jun. 17 DRAM bulletin summary says **CXMT’s re-inclusion on the US military list has no immediate sanctions**, but **Entity List restrictions continue to hinder advanced nodes**. CXMT is expanding server DRAM for domestic customers and building fabs, but foreign equipment limits constrain high-end progress.

## Demand side

- **AI/data center:** The HBM4E cycle is being pulled by next-gen AI accelerators. Korea Herald/TechTimes frame Nvidia’s **Rubin Ultra** as a key 2027 destination; TechTimes notes expected **384GB HBM per GPU** using eight 48GB stacks. Nvidia demand remains the central qualification prize.
- **Server DRAM:** TrendForce’s Jun. 9 server DRAM research summary says AI demand and proprietary chip adoption are driving upward revisions in server shipments; despite bottlenecks, memory faces **severe shortages and soaring prices**. DDR5 RDIMM spot **+16.28% weekly** supports that signal.
- **Smartphones:** TrendForce mobile/smartphone summaries say high memory costs are pressuring brands, allocations from Korean/US suppliers to smartphone clients are tighter, and global smartphone production is projected to decline **>10% YoY in 2026**. Smartphone inventory is described as healthy, but cost pressure is rising.
- **PC/OEM inventory:** TrendForce’s Jun. 12 foundry note says TV and PC/notebook supply chains accelerated production and inventory build for peripheral ICs in 1Q26. No fresh PC DRAM contract settlement detail was publicly accessible today.

## One-line outlook — next 1–2 weeks

- **Tightening / firm.** Spot DDR5 is already at **$46.00**, server RDIMM is still repricing sharply, and HBM/LPDRAM allocation into AI platforms is pulling capacity away from conventional and mobile DRAM. Watch for whether contract buyers accept another upward reset before month-end.
