## Spot & contract prices

- **DDR5 spot:** DRAMeXchange DDR5 16Gb 4800/5600 session average was **$46.733**, **flat day/day (0.00%)** as of Jun. 26 18:00 GMT+8. DDRWatcher also shows DDR5 **$46.7330** dated Jun. 26, with weekly regime **“Up Decel”** and no contract reset/module pass-through observed.
- **DDR4 spot:** DRAMeXchange DDR4 16Gb 3200 session average was **$72.317, +1.02% day/day**. DDR4 8Gb 3200 was **$36.00, flat**, while DDR4 8Gb eTT slipped **0.49%**.
- **LPDDR5/5X:** No fresh public spot print found today. Direction remains **tight / price-up pressure**: TrendForce’s June DRAM datasheet says high memory prices are compressing smartphone and notebook supply, cooling terminal procurement, while suppliers prioritize US CSPs and major chipmakers.
- **HBM3/HBM3E/HBM4:** No daily public HBM spot quote found. Status remains **sold-out/tight**; Micron reported HBM4 revenue **exceeded $1B** and said HBM4 12-high is ramping nearly **2x faster** than HBM3E 12-high. DRAMWatch still frames HBM3E as 2026 mainstream, HBM4 in mass/ramp, and HBM4E sampling/ramp into 2027.
- **Contracts:** Latest public TrendForce contract notes say PC/server DRAM contracts are moving **up**, supplier inventories remain low, and Q3/Q4 price-growth forecasts were revised upward. No fresh June contract-price table was publicly accessible in today’s pass.

## Supply side

- **Micron:** Latest earnings coverage shows memory tightness intensifying: revenue **$41.46B**, gross margin **84.9%**, net income **$28.24B**, and Q4 revenue guide about **$50B ± $1B**. Micron says tight market conditions likely persist **beyond 2027**; Q4 capex is projected around **$10B**.
- **Micron SCAs:** Micron has **16 strategic customer agreements** covering roughly **20% of DRAM output** and about **one-third of NAND** over the contract period; reported commitments include **$22B** and remaining performance obligations near **$100B**. These lock in volume/pricing floors and make near-term supply less available to open-market buyers.
- **Samsung / SK hynix / Micron HBM allocation:** DRAMWatch/TrendForce summary still shows HBM consuming about **23% of DRAM wafers in 2026E**, with SK hynix projected around **~50%** HBM bit share, Samsung **~28%**, Micron **~20%**; all three are prioritizing HBM/advanced nodes.
- **SK hynix capacity:** SK hynix’s long-term capacity plan is aggressive, but not near-term relief: Yongin DRAM expansion targets roughly **1M wafers/month by 2030** and **3x wafer output by 2034**; first Yongin facility expected early 2027, with meaningful output later.
- **China:** Micron acknowledged CXMT/YMTC progress, but noted the “overwhelming majority” of Chinese memory output remains domestic; China still trails Korea/US peers in HBM.

## Demand side

- **AI / data center:** This remains the main pull. Micron’s results/guidance and SCAs show hyperscaler/AI buyers locking supply years ahead. TrendForce says suppliers are prioritizing long-term demand from US CSPs and major chipmakers, causing prolonged negotiations with Chinese CSPs.
- **PC / consumer electronics:** Cost pass-through is broadening. TrendForce cites Lenovo viewing elevated memory prices as a possible “new normal” into 2030; earlier Lenovo reporting pointed to July price hikes and Dell server price hikes of **20%–40%**. Xbox says console storage/memory prices are up **>2.5x** and may double again by fall 2027, driving **$100–$150** console price hikes from Aug. 1.
- **Smartphone / notebook:** TrendForce’s June datasheet says high memory prices are compressing smartphone and notebook supply and cooling terminal procurement; notebook shipment forecasts were reportedly cut, so end demand is resisting price increases even as component supply remains tight.
- **OEM inventory:** Supplier inventories are described as **low / rock-bottom** in TrendForce’s contract notes; OEMs are trying to secure inventory before additional hikes, but spot buying is more cautious as asking prices rise.

## 1–2 week outlook

- **Tightening-to-firm.** DDR5 paused today, but DDR4 still rose and AI/HBM/long-term agreements are locking capacity; expect firm pricing with intermittent spot pauses rather than a clean pullback.
