## Spot & contract prices

- **DDR5:** TrendForce/DRAMeXchange spot session avg **$46.00** for DDR5 16Gb 4800/5600, **↑ 1.32%** session change. DDR5 16Gb eTT avg **$23.50**, **↑ 1.29%**.
- **DDR4:** TrendForce spot was mixed but mostly firm: DDR4 16Gb 3200 avg **$69.125**, **↑ 0.91%**; DDR4 8Gb 3200 avg **$35.90**, **↑ 0.28%**. eTT softened: DDR4 16Gb eTT **$11.80**, **↓ 0.63%**; DDR4 8Gb eTT **$4.45**, **↓ 0.58%**.
- **LPDDR5/5X:** No fresh public spot/contract print found in this run. Directional signal is **tight/upward cost pressure**: Apple flagged DRAM/memory cost inflation and said more DRAM supply is being allocated to HBM for AI servers.
- **HBM3/HBM3E/HBM4:** No public HBM spot price found. Status remains **allocation-tight / supplier-pricing-power biased**. SK hynix said it has begun shipping **12-high HBM4E samples** to major customers: **16Gbps per pin**, **48GB stack**, **>20% power-efficiency improvement** vs prior generation, and **17% better thermal performance** vs HBM4.
- **Contract prices:** No new DRAM contract reset found today. DDRWatcher flags **spot momentum = Up Accel**, but **contract reset = Not Seen** and **module pass-through = Not Seen**.

## Supply side

- **SK hynix:** Shipping 12-high HBM4E samples; moved HBM4E to **1c DRAM** from 1b for HBM4, aiming for higher density/power efficiency; using Advanced MR-MUF packaging.
- **Samsung:** TrendForce notes Samsung already delivered HBM4E samples in May and reportedly started HBM4 volume shipments in February; also targeting **1d DRAM** mass production as early as **1H27**.
- **Micron:** No material new Micron-specific production update found in the past 24–48h search results.
- **Capacity allocation:** TrendForce says major memory suppliers continue prioritizing higher-value products such as **HBM** and advanced-layer NAND, squeezing mature-node capacity; this is directly visible in adjacent NOR/SLC shortages and is consistent with DRAM cost pressure.

## Demand side

- **AI/data center:** AI server/HBM pull remains the dominant tightening driver; Apple specifically cited DRAM supply being redirected toward HBM for AI servers.
- **Smartphones/PCs:** Apple is preparing price hikes to offset memory costs; TrendForce estimates iPhone 18 Pro/Pro Max starting prices may rise **$150–200**, with possible earlier Mac/iPad increases. This points to memory inflation reaching consumer OEM pricing.
- **Hyperscalers/OEM inventories:** Hyperscaler AI demand remains aggressive enough that Apple is considering using cash reserves to secure memory supply. No fresh public OEM inventory percentage found.

## 1–2 week outlook

- **Tightening / upward bias.** DDR5 spot is accelerating, HBM/AI allocation is still pulling wafer capacity away from commodity DRAM, and no contract reset has appeared yet — so near-term spot pressure likely persists while contract/module pricing catches up more slowly.
