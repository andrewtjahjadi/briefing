## 💱 Markets & Macro

- **INR/USD:** Rupee settled around **₹94.34/$**, stronger than the prior close near **₹94.53/$**. The move was helped by softer crude and better inflows, while RBI dollar buying reportedly capped the rally.
- **Equities:** **Nifty 50 closed 24,168.00 (+0.34%)** and **Sensex closed 77,409.98 (+0.33%)**. PSU banks, healthcare, realty and consumer durables led; IT lagged.
- **10Y India government bond yield:** latest verifiable benchmark data was around **6.86–6.98%** depending on source/date cut; current clean daily close unavailable in this run.
- **RBI repo rate:** **5.25%**, after the latest MPC statement dated **5 Jun 2026**.
- **Oil:** Brent near **$78/bbl**. Lower crude matters directly for India’s CAD, inflation expectations and rupee stability.

## 📊 Economic Data & Policy

- **Direct taxes:** Net direct-tax collections rose **14.64% YoY to ₹5.21T** through Jun 17 in FY27; gross collections rose **12.46% to ₹6.1T**.
- **RBI liquidity:** RBI planned a **₹1T, 3-day VRR auction** as advance-tax outflows tightened system liquidity.
- **RBI / FX:** RBI reportedly bought dollars after recent rupee strength, using the window to rebuild reserves. FX reserves were reported around **$681.6B** for the week ended Jun 6.
- **Foreign-currency deposits:** RBI temporarily relaxed caps on longer-tenor **FCNR(B)** and **NRE** deposits through **Sep 30, 2026**, aiming to attract foreign-currency funding.
- **India–UK trade pact:** CETA and the Double Contribution Convention are set to take effect **Jul 15, 2026**, reducing tariffs across a broad set of goods while opening quota-based access in sensitive areas.

## 🏭 Business & Sector News

- **Banks / liquidity:** Public-sector banks led the equity tone as liquidity and flow measures supported financials.
- **Energy / crude-sensitive sectors:** Lower crude is positive for airlines, oil marketing companies, inflation and the rupee.
- **IT:** IT was the weakest major sector on the day, still pressured by global demand and currency cross-currents.
- **Digital / policy:** Foreign-currency deposit relaxation and trade-pact implementation are the highest-signal policy items for flows and business confidence.

## 📌 Bottom Line

India’s setup today is constructive but flow-dependent: lower crude is doing real work for the rupee and equities, while RBI is using the window to rebuild FX buffers. The policy signal is pro-inflow — FCNR/NRE relaxation plus the UK trade pact — but the market still needs durable portfolio inflows before the rupee rally becomes more than a relief move.
