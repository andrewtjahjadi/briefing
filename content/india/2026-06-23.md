## 💱 Markets & Macro

- **INR/USD:** RBI/FBIL showed **₹94.7064 per $1 at 1:00pm IST, Jun 23**; weaker versus the RBI homepage’s prior market reference around **₹94.49** from Jun 22.
- **Equities:** Sensex closed around **76,200, down 893 pts (~1.2%)**; Nifty 50 closed around **23,824, down 278 pts (~1.2%)**. The selloff was led by **IT and metals/tech**, with headlines citing global risk-off, rupee weakness, renewed FII outflows and profit-taking. Some midcaps still made 52-week highs, but breadth was defensive.
- **10Y India government bond:** direct 10Y close unavailable from the fetched sources; RBI’s government securities panel showed the **6.94% GS 2036 yield at 6.8468% as of Jun 22**. Treat exact 10Y as **n/a** today.
- **RBI repo rate:** **5.25%**, held unchanged at the **Jun 5, 2026** MPC decision; the RBI lowered FY27 GDP projection to **6.6%** and raised inflation projection to **5.1%**.
- **Oil:** Brent fell to about **$76.90/bbl, -1.28%** on Jun 23; softer oil is supportive for India’s import bill, inflation and external balance if sustained.

## 📊 Economic Data & Policy

- **Flash PMI cooled but stayed expansionary:** HSBC/S&P Global flash **Composite PMI fell to 57.4 in June from 59.3 in May**; **services fell to 57.3 from 59.8**, and **manufacturing eased to 54.5 from 55.0**. New orders grew at the slowest pace since March; employment growth softened; input and output price pressures cooled.
- **RBI external-liquidity support:** RBI clarified banks can extend loans against eligible **FCNR(B)** deposits raised through the swap facility; the special window runs for deposits mobilized through Sep 30, with facility availability up to Oct 16. This is meant to attract dollar deposits and ease pressure on the rupee/liquidity.
- **Credit regulation:** RBI moved to close arbitrage around **UPI-linked credit lines**, requiring the same prudential treatment as traditional credit facilities; it also allowed guarantee cover for TReDS receivables financing to support MSME working-capital flows.
- **Trade / tariffs:** India opened an **anti-dumping probe into electrical steel imports** from China, Japan, Korea and Russia after a domestic-industry complaint. WTO is also set to form a panel on China’s challenge to India’s solar and IT-goods measures.
- **Agriculture / monsoon risk:** Government is preparing contingency plans for **315 districts** as monsoon rains run materially below normal; ministers are pushing short-duration, water-efficient crops and water-harvesting repairs. This is the near-term food-inflation risk to watch.
- **Exports:** NITI Aayog said India’s services exports narrowed the gap with goods trade as overall trade grew **5.4% in Q4FY26**; details beyond the headline were not available in fetched text.

## 🏭 Business & Sector News

- **Autos:** Tata Motors plans to invest up to **₹40,000 crore by FY31**, targeting nearly doubled passenger-vehicle capacity, more models, and a **20% domestic market share**; EVs and CNG remain key growth legs. Bajaj Auto disclosed a ransomware attack affecting systems.
- **Aviation / infrastructure:** Adani Group plans **₹90,000 crore–₹1 lakh crore** of airport investment over five years and launched scheduled services from **Mundra** to Mumbai/Goa; Akasa Air is targeting **30% capacity growth in FY27**.
- **Telecom:** Bharti Airtel increased its Airtel Africa stake to about **79%** via a roughly **₹28,200 crore** share-swap; Vodafone Idea raised **₹1,182 crore** from promoter Aditya Birla Group; DoT kept backhaul spectrum pricing unchanged despite TRAI’s cut proposal.
- **Banking / credit:** Experian reported gold loans’ share of retail credit rising from **18% in FY23 to 41% in FY26** as gold prices surged. Kotak Mahindra Bank signaled appetite for acquisitions/loan portfolios while investing in AI and tech.
- **Manufacturing / exports:** The Centre is coordinating states and industry around a **$100 billion textile and apparel export target by 2030**, with focus on technical textiles, man-made fibres, logistics and district-led value addition.
- **Energy / commodities:** Softer Brent is a macro tailwind; ET cited Hormuz shipping activity recovering and Indian refiners benefiting amid disruption. Renewable/EV headlines were active, including VinFast India’s Shriram Finance tie-up for vehicle financing.

## 📌 Bottom Line

- India’s growth pulse is still expansionary, but June flash PMI shows cooling demand, softer hiring and weaker confidence — not a crisis, but momentum is less clean.
- Markets sold off hard as rupee weakness, IT/metals pressure and global risk-off hit equities; softer Brent is the main macro offset.
- Watch monsoon progress and RBI’s dollar-attraction measures: food inflation and currency stability are the two practical swing factors for the next few weeks.
