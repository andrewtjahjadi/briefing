## Headline

- **GDP:** real GDP fell **0.1% q/q** in Q1 2026 after **+0.2%** in Q4 2025; household consumption fell **0.2%**, GFCF dropped **0.6%**, and net trade subtracted **0.9pp** from growth.
- **Inflation:** May CPI rose **2.4% y/y** and **0.1% m/m**; HICP inflation accelerated to **2.8% y/y**, while core inflation rose to **1.5% y/y**.
- **Central-bank view:** the Banque de France published its June projections on **17 Jun 2026**, keeping the policy debate focused on subdued growth, inflation pass-through, and the next two-year macro path.

## Industrial / trade signal

- **Manufacturing:** April manufacturing output increased **0.4% m/m** after **+1.3%** in March, while whole-industry output slowed to **+0.1% m/m** after **+1.4%**.
- **Transport equipment:** manufacturing gains were led by transport equipment, where the index rose to **129.67** in April from **125.52** in March — a useful offset after Q1 GDP was hit by weaker aeronautics exports.
- **Foreign trade drag:** Q1 exports fell **3.5%** after **+0.9%**, while imports declined **0.9%**; the GDP accounts flagged aeronautics as the main export drag.

## Labor & consumer

- **Unemployment:** the ILO unemployment rate rose **0.2pp** in Q1 to **8.1%**, with unemployed people increasing by **68,000** to **2.6M**; unemployment is now **0.7pp** higher than a year ago.
- **Wages:** hourly wages in the non-agricultural market sector rose **2.0% y/y** in Q1, while hourly labour costs rose **2.3% y/y**; quarterly wage growth slowed to **0.3%**.
- **Household goods demand:** April household consumption of goods fell **0.5% m/m** after **+0.9%** in March, led by a **2.9%** fall in energy consumption.
- **Confidence:** the INSEE business-climate indicator stayed weak at **94** in May, below its **100** long-term average; the employment-climate indicator fell to **92**, its weakest level since the health crisis.

## 1–2 week outlook

- **Cautious / sideways bias.** France has a better April factory print, but the broader signal is still soft: Q1 contraction, rising unemployment, weak business climate, and higher energy-led inflation limit near-term upside.
- **Watch points:** June flash PMIs/business surveys, energy-price pass-through, aeronautics export normalization, and whether fiscal/political headlines reinforce or relieve pressure on household and business confidence.
