## 💱 Markets & Macro

- **IDR/USD:** ~**Rp17,863/USD** latest Yahoo quote, weaker vs prior daily close around Rp17,788/USD; President Prabowo publicly tied long-run rupiah weakness to capital outflows, citing **US$436bn** cumulative trade surplus over 22 years and saying ~**78% / US$343bn** flowed abroad.
- **JCI/IHSG:** latest Yahoo quote **6,101.33** vs prior close **6,116.69** (**~0.25% lower**). Sector movers: **n/a** — IDX sector board was not retrievable from public source during this run.
- **10Y INDOGB:** **7.22%**, up **9 bps** from the previous session, per Trading Economics OTC quotes.
- **Bank Indonesia:** BI raised the **BI-Rate 25 bps to 5.75%** at the **17–18 Jun 2026** Board of Governors meeting; DF **4.75%**, LF **6.50%**. BI framed it as rupiah-stability and inflation-target protection.
- **Oil:** Brent ~**US$76.82/bbl**, WTI ~**US$72.93/bbl** latest Yahoo futures quotes, materially lower on the day; Finance Minister Purbaya said easing US-Iran tensions could lower non-subsidized Pertamax prices.

## 📊 Economic Data & Policy

- **Money supply:** BI reported **May M2 +10.8% y/y** to **Rp10,415.9tn**, accelerating from **+9.2% y/y** in April; M1 **+15.3%**, quasi-money **+6.0%**; disbursed loans **+10.8% y/y** vs **+9.4%** in April.
- **Growth / inflation context:** Finance Minister Purbaya said **Q1 GDP +5.61% y/y**, **May CPI +3.08% y/y**, FX reserves **US$144.9bn** / **5.6 months of imports**, trade surplus streak **72 months through Apr 2026**, lending **+11.5% y/y**, unemployment **4.68%**.
- **BI policy plumbing:** From **1 Jul 2026**, BI will lift the Bank Foreign Funding Ratio cap to **40% of bank capital** from **35%**; lower the no-underlying FX cash purchase threshold to **US$10,000/person/month**; and lower outgoing FX-transfer supporting-doc threshold to **US$25,000** from **US$50,000**.
- **Fuel / subsidies:** Purbaya said lower global crude could feed through to **Pertamax and other non-subsidized fuels**, while subsidized fuels remain protected; this is positive for H2 purchasing power if oil stays lower.
- **Financial-sector reform:** Government proposed an **Indonesia International Financial Center (IIFC)** bill for the 2026 legislative program; the bill is tied to Law No. 4/2026 and must be enacted within three months of **17 Jun 2026**.
- **Financing / FX:** Indonesia is targeting an initial **US$1bn Panda Bond** in China, with possible upsizing if demand supports it; proceeds are framed as development financing and rupiah-stability diversification.
- **Trade / industrial policy:** Pupuk Indonesia is exporting urea under a **250,000-tonne** G-to-G deal with Australia and is in talks with **India, Bangladesh, and other Asian buyers** after domestic farmer needs are met.

## 🏭 Business & Sector News

- **SOE/BUMN / Danantara:** Prabowo said the government aims to dissolve **700–800** underperforming SOE entities, after already liquidating about **240** loss-making businesses; BP BUMN’s consolidation target is to reduce state firms/subsidiaries from **~1,100 to 257**. Danantara now carries operational responsibility over state assets after the SOE ministry overhaul.
- **Infrastructure:** In Sampang, East Java, Prabowo inaugurated **1,151 km** of regional roads across **37 provinces**, funded by **Rp5.41tn** of state budget; stated policy goal is lower logistics costs and stronger food/energy distribution.
- **Downstreaming / energy:** Setkab said Prabowo received reports from Energy Minister Bahlil and other ministers on downstream industrial development, energy security, national electricity stability, and EV battery ecosystem progress.
- **Labor watch:** Manpower Ministry is monitoring layoff risks via a dashboard; flagged cases include a Bekasi ceramics factory with potential exposure of **>50,000 workers** linked to industrial gas prices, plus auto-component risks in East Java.
- **Digital economy:** Communications Ministry launched **DEAL 2026**, with eight collaboration packages covering connectivity, consumer protection, startups, AI, digital inclusion, logistics-cost efficiency, and a national digital ecosystem dashboard.
- **Industrial diplomacy:** Industry Ministry said **50 Indonesian industrial companies** will attend INNOPROM 2026 in Yekaterinburg on **6–9 Jul**, with a **1,500+ sqm** pavilion covering agro/food, chemicals/pharma, advanced manufacturing, industrial estates, and investment.

## 📌 Bottom Line

- today’s macro story is **policy tightening plus FX defense**: BI is leaning harder against rupiah pressure while trying to keep credit channels open.
- The political-economy story is **state-asset cleanup**: Prabowo is pushing SOE consolidation through Danantara/BP BUMN, with corruption and fiscal leakage as the public rationale.
- Lower oil is a near-term relief valve for Indonesia’s fuel-price, inflation, and subsidy math — but jobs risk in ceramics/autos is the domestic stress point to watch.
