## 💱 Markets & Macro
- **IDR/USD:** ~**Rp17,950/USD** latest Yahoo chart quote, weaker from ~**Rp17,862/USD** prior daily close (**~0.5% IDR depreciation**). Direction still points to FX pressure despite BI’s latest rate hike and intervention-heavy stance.
- **JCI/IHSG:** Yahoo chart showed **5,883.88** at the Jakarta close, down from **6,101.33** on Jun 23 (**~3.6% lower**); day range **5,876.93–6,171.38**. Sector movers: **n/a** — IDX sector board was blocked/unavailable in this run.
- **10Y INDOGB:** **7.17%**, down **5 bps** from **7.22%** previous session, per Trading Economics OTC quotes.
- **Bank Indonesia:** BI-Rate **5.75%**, Deposit Facility **4.75%**, Lending Facility **6.50%** after the **17–18 Jun 2026** Board of Governors meeting; BI framed the 25 bp hike as rupiah stabilization and inflation-target defense.
- **Oil:** Brent ~**US$74.18/bbl**, WTI ~**US$70.58/bbl**, both materially lower on the day; relevant for Indonesia’s fuel-import/subsidy math and the B50 biodiesel push.

## 📊 Economic Data & Policy
- **Money supply:** BI reported **May M2 +10.8% y/y** to **Rp10,415.9tn**, accelerating from **+9.2% y/y** in April. Components: M1 **+15.3%**, quasi-money **+6.0%**; disbursed loans **+10.8% y/y** vs **+9.4%** in April; net foreign assets **+5.2% y/y** vs **+3.7%**.
- **Latest macro prints:** No new CPI/trade/GDP release found in the last 24h. Latest public dashboard items remain **May CPI 3.08% y/y** and BI’s inflation target **2.5% ±1%**; agriculture was cited at **12.67% of Q1 2026 GDP**, behind manufacturing **19.07%** and trade **13.28%**.
- **BI policy plumbing:** From **1 Jul 2026**, BI will raise the Bank Foreign Funding Ratio cap to **40% of bank capital** from **35%**, lower the no-underlying FX cash-purchase threshold to **US$10,000/person/month**, and lower the outgoing FX-transfer documentation threshold to **US$25,000** from **US$50,000**.
- **Food / agriculture:** Government allocated **Rp14tn** for nationwide irrigation revitalization in **2026**, up from **Rp12tn**, with the program running through **2029**; stated goal is higher cropping intensity and food self-sufficiency.
- **Housing:** Government approved a **40-year** subsidized KPR mortgage tenor, maintained landed-house subsidized mortgage rates at **5%** despite the BI hike, set apartment subsidized mortgage rates at **6%**, and prepared a **350,000-unit** subsidized housing quota.
- **Energy / subsidies:** Prabowo said Indonesia will launch **B50 biodiesel in July**, targeting diesel-import elimination; Energy Minister Bahlil previously said the launch is expected **1 Jul 2026** after trials on heavy equipment, ships, trains, mining machinery, excavators, and farm equipment. Government estimate: up to **Rp157.28tn** in FX savings this year.
- **Investment positioning:** Coordinating Minister Airlangga Hartarto pitched Indonesia as a safe-haven FDI destination amid geopolitical turmoil, citing ASEAN growth above **4%**, high SEZ occupancy, fiscal-monetary coordination, and OECD accession as investor-confidence levers.

## 🏭 Business & Sector News
- **Digital infrastructure:** Airlangga said data centers are a key lever for the administration’s **>8%** growth ambition, tied to AI and the digital economy. He also cited ASEAN DEFA, Indonesia’s land/energy advantages, ARM-linked semiconductor design cooperation, and a target to train **15,000** semiconductor talents over three years.
- **EV battery / downstreaming:** Indonesia is widening its EV battery strategy from production to recycling and waste management. Policymakers flagged **~103,000 EV units sold in 2025**, LFP batteries at **~96%** of Indonesia’s 2024 electric-car battery usage, nickel-manganese batteries at **~4%**, and global EV battery demand rising from **~1 TWh in 2024** to **~3 TWh by 2030**.
- **Agriculture / fertilizer:** Indonesia delivered **47,250 metric tons** of urea to Australia under the broader **250,000-tonne** G-to-G fertilizer deal; the government frames this as food-security diplomacy after meeting domestic farmer needs.
- **Palm oil / biodiesel:** B50 is the biggest commodity-policy signal today: it supports domestic palm-oil absorption and reduces fuel imports, but will keep the palm-oil balance sheet politically sensitive if feedstock prices rise.
- **SOE/BUMN / Danantara:** No new Danantara transaction found today; carry-forward watch item remains Prabowo’s plan to dissolve **700–800** underperforming SOE entities and consolidate state assets under the post-SOE-ministry structure.
- **Commodities watch:** Nickel/copper/coal/LNG: **n/a** for new high-signal public items in the last 24h from retrieved sources.

## 📌 Bottom Line
- today is a **rupiah-and-risk day**: BI tightened last week, but USD/IDR is still near **17,950** and JCI had a sharp drawdown.
- Prabowo is leaning hard into **self-sufficiency politics** — food, irrigation, B50 biodiesel, and subsidized housing — while using Gorontalo to message directly to farmers and fishers.
- The B50 rollout is the key policy-market bridge: bullish for palm-oil absorption and FX savings if implementation works, but it adds execution risk to fuel logistics and feedstock pricing.
