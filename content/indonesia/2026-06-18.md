## 💱 Markets & Macro

- **IDR/USD:** USD/IDR around **17,874**, up about **0.13%** versus the prior session — rupiah weaker despite Bank Indonesia’s rate action.
- **JCI / IHSG:** closed around **6,172.34 (-0.78%)**. Risk tone was soft; financials, infrastructure, industrials and energy were among the laggards.
- **10Y INDOGB yield:** around **6.97%**, roughly **+6 bps** versus the prior session.
- **BI-Rate / 7-Day Reverse Repo:** Bank Indonesia hiked **25 bps to 5.75%** at the **17–18 Jun 2026** RDG. Deposit Facility is **4.75%** and Lending Facility **6.50%**.
- **Oil:** Brent near **$78/bbl** and WTI near **$75/bbl**. The drop matters because Indonesia is exposed to subsidy and import-bill pressure.

## 📊 Economic Data & Policy

- **Inflation:** May CPI was **3.08% YoY**, up from **2.42%** in April and above consensus around **2.97%**; core CPI was **2.59%**.
- **Trade:** April trade surplus narrowed sharply to around **$0.09B**, far below expectations and down from **$3.32B** in March; imports and exports both rose more than 20% YoY.
- **BI policy package:** BI paired the hike with FX intervention, attractive SRBI yields, a **10% hedging-swap incentive** for foreign investors, repo-window support and a higher foreign-funding ratio cap for banks.
- **FX prudential tightening:** BI will lower cash FX purchases without underlying to **$10,000/person/month** and tighten outbound FX-transfer documentation thresholds from **1 Jul 2026**.
- **Demand indicators:** Consumer confidence remained optimistic at **120.9** in May. BI’s retail-sales index pointed to improving monthly momentum after April weakness.

## 🏭 Business & Sector News

- **Banks / rupiah:** The rate hike and FX controls are the day’s dominant business signal. They stabilize the currency but keep pressure on credit-sensitive sectors.
- **Energy / subsidies:** Lower crude is helpful, but the fiscal effect depends on whether prices stay down and whether subsidy settings remain unchanged.
- **Commodities:** Nickel, coal and palm oil remain key external-risk channels; today’s market move was more FX/rates-led than single-commodity-led.
- **Consumption:** The free-meals program and broader fiscal support remain important watchpoints for demand and budget credibility.

## 📌 Bottom Line

Indonesia is in currency-defense mode. BI’s hike and FX controls are a serious stabilization package, but the rupiah still weakened, which tells you the market wants proof that policy can outrun external pressure. Softer oil is the best near-term relief valve; without it, subsidy and FX pressure would look uglier.
