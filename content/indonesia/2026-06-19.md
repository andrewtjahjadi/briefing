## 💱 Markets & Macro

- **IDR/USD:** USD/IDR last **17,793** on Jun 19, **+0.16% d/d** — rupiah weaker on the day after strengthening near **17,700** in the prior session; still about **0.6% stronger w/w**. BI also reported rupiah at **Rp17,730/USD on Jun 17**, up **0.76% point-to-point** from end-May, helped by intervention and SRBI/SBN inflows.
- **JCI / IHSG:** **6,177.14**, **+0.08% d/d** on Jun 19. Intraday pressure was broad — industrials, financials, property, and basic materials lagged; named decliners included **Telkom Indonesia (-6.8%)**, **Sarana Menara Nusantara (-6.5%)**, **Barito Pacific (-4.2%)**, and **Bank Mandiri (-3.4%)**.
- **10Y INDOGB:** **7.07%**, **+10 bp d/d**.
- **Bank Indonesia:** BI-Rate **5.75%** after the **17–18 Jun 2026** Board of Governors meeting; BI raised the BI-Rate, Deposit Facility, and Lending Facility by **25 bp** to **5.75% / 4.75% / 6.50%**.
- **Oil:** Brent **$80.58/bbl (+0.91% d/d)**; WTI **$77.24/bbl (+0.84% d/d)**. Both are still sharply lower m/m after the Hormuz/US-Iran risk premium eased, which matters for Indonesia’s fuel-subsidy and import bill.

## 📊 Economic Data & Policy

- **Inflation:** May CPI **3.08% y/y** vs **2.42% prior** and **2.97% consensus**; m/m CPI **0.28%** vs **0.13% prior** and **0.14% forecast**. Core inflation rose to **2.59%** from **2.44%**. Food was the main pressure point, with food inflation **4.94%** vs **3.06%** in April.
- **Trade:** April trade surplus narrowed to **$0.09bn** vs **$3.32bn prior** and **$1.5bn consensus**. Imports rose **22.49% y/y**; exports rose **21.98% y/y**. BI’s policy statement also flagged the April goods surplus at about **$0.1bn**, down from **$3.3bn** in March.
- **PMI:** May S&P Global manufacturing PMI recovered to **50.0** from **49.1**, i.e., flat/stabilized factory conditions; export orders still contracted and input-cost inflation remained severe.
- **FX reserves:** May reserves **$144.9bn**, down from **$146.2bn**, equivalent to **5.6 months of imports** or **5.5 months incl. government external-debt service**.
- **External debt:** April external debt **$439.8bn**, growth **1.9% y/y** vs **1.0%** in March; external debt/GDP held at **29.6%** and long-term debt was **84.5%** of total.
- **BI policy mix:** BI said the rate hike is a pre-emptive stability move to keep inflation inside the **2.5% ±1%** target band in 2026–27, with heavier FX intervention, SRBI yield support, hedging-swap incentives for non-residents, and tighter FX documentation/cash-purchase rules effective **Jul 1**.
- **Fiscal / financing:** Finance Minister Purbaya said Indonesia is targeting an initial **$1bn Panda Bond** in China and has an **AIIB $17bn multi-year loan facility through 2029** available for infrastructure. Danantara’s inaugural global bond raised **$1.5bn**, per Setkab.
- **Fuel / subsidies:** Officials are watching whether lower oil after the Hormuz reopening filters into non-subsidized fuel prices; Antara notes Indonesia imports about **1.6m bpd** of oil, so sustained oil declines would improve fiscal room.
- **MBG / free meals:** Market chatter still treats funding cuts/holiday suspension as part of the fiscal-risk debate; no fresh official budget number found in the last 24h.

## 🏭 Business & Sector News

- **State banks / Danantara / exports:** Prabowo told Himbara state banks to broaden public impact and support growth, including a planned **one-stop export system** where export FX earnings are managed centrally through **PT Danantara Sumberdaya Indonesia (DSI)** and channeled through Himbara.
- **Palm oil / biodiesel:** Indonesia says **B50 biodiesel** is on track for **Jul 1, 2026**. ESDM projects up to **Rp157.28tn / $8.84bn** in FX savings this year, **Rp24.68tn / $1.39bn** CPO added value, **2.21m** workers absorbed, and **46.72m tons** GHG reduction. Separately, the Agriculture Ministry says about **90%** of palm-oil companies have raised fresh-fruit-bunch prices after tighter oversight; firms still under investigation fell from about **270** to **130**.
- **Nickel / China:** Chinese investors flagged concerns over Indonesian policy changes, including a reported **70% reduction in nickel ore mining quotas**, royalty hikes, stricter export-revenue retention, and visa processes. Purbaya said Prabowo would improve the investment climate while maintaining fair treatment.
- **China investment:** China invested **$5.4bn** in Indonesia in 2025 (**+13.5% y/y**) and **$2.2bn** in Q1 2026 (**+22% y/y**), with nearly half going into downstream industry; China ranked third after Singapore and Hong Kong.
- **Energy security:** Indonesia and Kuwait opened cooperation talks on energy security; this sits alongside the government’s oil-source diversification push after the Hormuz shock.
- **Coal / minerals:** No fresh confirmed coal-export-rule change today; the week’s policy thread remains DSI-led resource-export centralization and ESDM’s tighter RKAB/critical-mineral management.

Public official items reviewed this week were Jakarta/Hambalang meetings — German President Steinmeier’s Jakarta state visit, Qatar minister courtesy call, hajj meeting at Hambalang, and agriculture/food-stock meeting — not counted as travel entries.

## 📌 Bottom Line

- The core signal is **policy tightening for rupiah defense**: BI hiked again to **5.75%**, rates/yields are up, and growth support is being pushed through macroprudential/liquidity channels instead.
- Indonesia’s external accounts are the pressure point: April trade surplus nearly vanished, reserves fell, and oil/fuel sensitivity remains high despite the current oil-price relief.
- Prabowo’s economic operating model is becoming clearer: **state banks + Danantara/DSI + export FX centralization + downstreaming**, with China investor concerns now important to watch.
